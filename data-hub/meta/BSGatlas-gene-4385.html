Comment LipL encodes an amidotransferase that is required for lipoate biosynthesis in B. subtilis |CITS: [21338420][21338421]|. The lipL gene was cloned with a hexahistidine-tag, expressed in E. coli and purified to homogeneity |CITS: [21338421]|. The purified protein could bind up to two molecules of either |FRAME: LIPOIC-ACID| or |FRAME: CPD-195|. These molecules do not bind to lysine residues, as they do in the lipoyl domains of 2-oxo acid dehydrogenases, but to two cysteine residues, located at positions C39 and C150. Mutation analysis demonstrated that only C150 is essential for activity, and the authors suspect that acylation of C39 may have been an artifact. The enzyme was shown to catalyze amidotransfer of octanoate from an octanoylated GcvH protein (the lipoate binding component of the glycine cleavage system) to the homologous component of the pyruvate dehydrogenase complex, via an octanoyl-enzyme intermediate. It can not accept free octanoate as a substrate, and thus GcvH is a required intermediate in the lipoylation of other 2-oxo acid dehydrogenases in B. subtilis |CITS: [21338421]|. One or more strains containing mutant alleles of this gene can be obtained from the Bacillus Genetic Stock Center. Click here to see the list of available strains at BGSC.

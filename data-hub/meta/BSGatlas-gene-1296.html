Comment |FRAME: TAX-1423| is able to grow using |FRAME: CPD-8050| as its sole carbon source. In order to identify the enzyme(s) that operate on this compound, the genome was analyzed for genes paralogous to |FRAME: BSU39700|, which encodes |FRAME: CPLX-8649|. Seven such genes were identified, and two of them were prominently expressed when the cells were grown on medium containing |FRAME: CPD-8050|. The two genes, |FRAME: BSU33530| and |FRAME: BSU10850|, were cloned in |FRAME:TAX-562| as C-terminally His6-tagged fusion proteins, and the recombinant proteins were purified |CITS: [20133360]|. Both proteins were shown to encode a functional |FRAME: CPD-8050| dehydrogenase enzyme. |FRAME: BSU10850| encodes a catabolic NAD+-dependent enzyme, while |FRAME: BSU33530| encodes a presumably biosynthetic NADP+-dependent enzyme |CITS: [20133360]|. Both enzymes possess a much reduced activity with |FRAME: MYO-INOSITOL|. Inactivation of |FRAME: BSU10850|, but not |FRAME: BSU33530|, abolished growth on |FRAME: CPD-8050| |CITS: [20133360]|.

The culture was then incubated in a 500 ml baffled flask with shaking at 250 rpm [aero]. Anaerobic growth was carried out (i) in the presence of 10 mM potassium nitrate (nitrate respiration) [nit]; or (ii) in the absence of 10 mM postassium nitrate (fermentative growth) [ferm]. The procedure for anaerobic growth was: medium was inoculated to an OD578 nm of 0.1 in flasks completely filled with medium and sealed with rubber stoppers. They were shaken at 100 rpm to minimize cell aggregation. These cultures were inoculated aerobically with an aerobically grown overnight culture. Anaerobic conditions were achieved in the stoppered flasks after a short time through the consumption of residual oxygen. Cells were harvested during the exponential growth phase.

Comment The |FRAME: BSU35760| gene of |FRAME:TAX-224308| was proposed to catalyze a priming reaction, attaching the first glycerol phosphate unit to the disccharide of the teichoic acid linkage unit. This attachment is required before the |FRAME: BSU35720 TagF| polymerase can add successive glycerol phosphate units to the nascent chain. TagB has a strong sequence similarity to TagF, which has been confirmed to catalyze the polymerization reaction |CITS: [12637499]|, supports this hypothesis |CITS: [11882717]|. The |FRAME: BSU35760| gene was cloned with a poly-His tag and expressed in |FRAME: TAX-562|, and the recombinant protein was purified |CITS: [17163636]|. A substrate for the enzyme was prepared by incubating purified |FRAME: BSU35750 TagA| enzyme with a synthetic substrate that contains a synthetic 13-carbon saturated lipid chain instead of the natural 55-carbon undecaprenyl. When purified TagB protein was incubated with this substrate and CDP-glycerol, it produced the expected prduct, which was confirmed by NMR |CITS: [17163636]|.

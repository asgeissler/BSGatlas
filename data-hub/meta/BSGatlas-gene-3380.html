The gene is monocistronic and is transcribed from sigma(A)-like promoter. Expression is induced by arabinose and arabinan and is repressed by glucose. The induction mechanism is mediated through negative control by the key regulator of arabinose metabolism, AraR |CITS: [14973026]|. The gene was cloned with a His6-tag fused to the C-terminus and expressed in E. coli. The recombinant protein, which is extracellular in its native host, was secreted to the periplasmic space and correctly processed by the E. coli signal peptidase. The recombinant protein was purified to homogeneity |CITS: [15556708]|. The enzyme consists of a glycoside hydrolase family 43 (GH43) catalytic domain. The purified enzyme was active towards linear |FRAME: L-Arabinans L-arabinan| and branched sugar beet arabinan, but inactive towards larch wood |FRAME: Arabinogalactan arabinogalactan|, wheat |FRAME: Arabinoxylan arabinoxylan| and |FRAME: CPD-13294|. Linear arabinan is degraded faster than branched arabinan |CITS: [15556708]|. E215 is a key residue for AbnA hydrolytic activity. A mutant in which E215 was converted to alanine had no activity.

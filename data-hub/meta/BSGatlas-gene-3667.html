Comment RhaEW is a novel (as of 2013) bifunctional enzyme catalyzing two consecutive steps in |FRAME: L-rhamnose| catabolism, |FRAME: EC-4.1.2.19| and |FRAME: EC-1.2.1.22|. The gene encoding the enzyme was identified in diverse bacterial lineages including Actinobacteria, Î±-proteobacteria, Bacilli, Bacteroidetes, and Chloroflexi |CITS: [24391637]|. The N-terminal domain of the enzyme from |FRAME: TAX-1423| is 14% identical to RhaD from |FRAME: TAX-562|, while the C-terminal domain is homologous to various NADH-and NADPH-dependent sugar dehydrogenases. The |FRAME: BSU31220| gene was cloned from |FRAME: TAX-1423| with an N-terminal His6 tags and expressed in |FRAME: TAX-562|, and the recombinant protein was purified |CITS: [24391637]|. To assay the purified protein, purified recombinant |FRAME: EC-5.3.1.14| (RhaA) from |FRAME: TAX-562| and |FRAME: EC-2.7.1.5| (RhaB) form |FRAME: TAX-2336| were pre-incubated with |FRAME: L-rhamnose|, producing |FRAME: RHAMNULOSE-1P| (which is not available commercially). The RhaEW enzyme was then added, and the reaction was followed by monitoring the reduction of NAD+. The identity of the products was confirmed by GC-MS |CITS: [24391637]|. The function of the enzyme was further validated by creating a knockout mutant. The mutant lost the ability to utilize |FRAME: L-rhamnose| |CITS: [24391637]|.

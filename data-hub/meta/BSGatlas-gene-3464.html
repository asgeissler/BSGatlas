Comment In E. coli ThiI is a bifunctional enzyme that is required both for the synthesis of the thiazole moiety of thiamin and for the conversion of uridine to 4-thiouridine at position 8 in tRNA. The protein is proposed to accept a sulfur from IscS-persulfide to give a ThiI-persulfide, which is subsequently used in the biosynthesis of thiazole phosphate and thiouridine |CITS: [6178725][9592144]|. However, the B. subtilis ThiI is missing the 120-amino acid carboxy terminal domain containing the proposed redox active cysteine residue in the E. coli enzyme. Addition of purified ThiI protein to to a thiazole reconstitution reaction mixture did not result in any enhancement in the rate of thiazole phosphate formation |CITS: [14567704]|. Therefore, it is currently not certain whether the enzyme plays any role in thiazole biosynthesis in B. subtilis.

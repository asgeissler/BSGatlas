Comment B. subtilis is able to grow using |FRAME: CPD-8050| as its sole carbon source. In order to identify the enzyme(s) that operate on this compound, the genome was analyzed for genes paralogous to |FRAME: BSU39700|, which encodes |FRAME: CPLX-8649|. Seven such genes were identified, and two of them were prominently expressed when the cells were grown on medium containing |FRAME: CPD-8050|. The two genes, |FRAME: BSU33530| and |FRAME: BSU10850|, were cloned in |FRAME:TAX-562| as C-terminally His6-tagged fusion proteins, and the recombinant proteins were purified |CITS: [20133360]|. Both proteins were shown to encode a functional |FRAME: CPD-8050| dehydrogenase enzyme. |FRAME: BSU10850| encodes a catabolic NAD+-dependent enzyme, while |FRAME: BSU33530| encodes a presumably biosynthetic NADP+-dependent enzyme |CITS: [20133360]|. Both enzymes possess a much reduced activity with |FRAME: MYO-INOSITOL|. Inactivation of |FRAME: BSU10850|, but not |FRAME: BSU33530|, abolished growth on |FRAME: CPD-8050| |CITS: [20133360]|.

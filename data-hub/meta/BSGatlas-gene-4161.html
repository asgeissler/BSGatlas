Comment Although initially annotated as regulatory proteins, the |FRAME: BSU35840|, |FRAME: BSU35650| and |FRAME: BSU35520| genes encode phosphotransferases that catalyze the final step in the assembly of anionic cell wall polymers - the transferring of teichoic acids and acidic capsular polysaccharides from their lipid-linked precursors to cell wall peptidoglycan |CITS: [21964069]|. All three gene are located within a 50 kpb segment of the chromosome that contains all of the known all teichoic acid biosynthesis genes. The three proteins belong to a protein family named LCP (LytR-Cps2A-Psr) whose members are found in all Gram-positive bacteria that produce anionic polymers. Since the three proteins have overlapping activities, a mutation in only one of them does not result in a phenotype. However, a ΔtagTV double mutant grows more slowly than the wild type and has a wider cell shape, and a ΔtagTUV triple mutant is lethal. When the |FRAME: BSU35520| gene was placed under control of an IPTG-dependent promoter in a ΔtagTU double mutant, removing IPTG from the medium resulted in gradual growth arrest, accompanied by cell morphology changes - the cells became shorter and wider with noticeable bulging, and eventually became almost round |CITS: [21964069]|. Crystalization of a related protein from |FRAME: TAX-1313| (|FRAME: G-44730 Cps2A|), expressed without its transmembrane domain in |FRAME: TAX-562|, revealed that the protein was purified with an isopernoid phosphate lipid in its hydrophobic pocket. The enzyme behaved as a phosphotransferase, catalyzing the Mg2+-dependent hydrolysis of the diphosphate phosphoester bond and releasing orthophosphate |CITS: [21964069]|. While purified TagT expressed without its transmembrane domain does not contain a lipid, it is able to catalyze the hydrolysis of the diphosphate phosphoester bond in an exogenously provided |FRAME: GERANYL-PP| indicating that it too is a phosphotransferase. Based on all of these observation, it has been [roposed that the three proteins are the long-sought enzymes that catalyze the final step in wall teichoic acid biosynthesis - the transfer of the acids from their lipid anchor to the peptidoglycan |CITS: [21964069]|.

(MG-0.2) A culture of LB medium was inocualted from a frozen glycerol stock of B. subtilis. After few hours at 37oC when the culture was growing exponentially, this culture was used to inoculate M9 minimal medium at several different dilutions usually in the range of 500- to 2000-fold. The dilution range was chosen to ensure that at least one of these M9 precultures had reached an OD600 between 0.5 - 1.0 after overnight incubation. These precultures were then used to inoculate 2.5 L of M9 medium in a 3.1 L KLF bioreactor (Bioengineering AG, Wald, Switzerland) to a starting OD600 of 0.03 – 0.05. Condiions in the bioreactor were rigorously controlled as follows: temperature was controlled at 37 °C; the pH was maintained at exactly 7.2 by automatic titration with 2.0 M KOH and 2.0 M H2SO4, and the dissolved oxygen tension was maintained above 50%. In each nutritional shift experiment cells were grown on the single substrate until the OD600 reached 0.50, at which point the second substrate was added instantaneously (4 g/L L-malate or 3 g/L glucose). The nutrient shifts performed were from glucose to glucose+malate [GM] and from malate to malate+glucose [MG] (Buescher et al., accompanying paper). Cell growth during the course was monitored throughout the experiment by measuring OD600.
